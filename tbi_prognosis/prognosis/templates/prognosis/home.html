About GCS and GOS:

The Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) is a neurological scale used to measure a patient's level of consciousness after a traumatic brain injury. It evaluates three key areas: eye-opening response, verbal response, and motor response, with scores ranging from 3 (deep unconsciousness) to 15 (fully awake).

The Glasgow Outcome Scale (GOS) helps assess recovery outcomes by categorizing patients into levels such as mild disability, moderate disability, severe disability, or death. Together, these scales provide critical insights into a patient's condition and guide decision-making for treatment.
